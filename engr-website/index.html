Style Guide

Approach to Plain Language:

Header Format:

The headers of section within a main topic are 18 bold font chosen to give the reader distinction about the segments discussed throughout the main topic. Additionally, the main topic is 24 bold font. This is the largest font used to indicate sections of overarching importance and the main theme. The authors of the writing’s name always appear in 12 font in the left top corner of the page. The date the article was published/written always appears in the top right corner in italicized 12 font. These locations were chosen for easy reference. The font size is size 12 to prevent drawing attention to the lower significant items on the informative page.


Poster Standards:

Document Standards:

For our font choice, we we want something visually appealing and tasteful, however, not distracting. We have chosen 14 point font for regular text, 18 and bold for headers and bold, and 24, bold, and centered for titles. Key words in our glossary will be underlined in order to draw attention to the term and hope it sticks with the reader. The margins will be 0.75” x 0.75”. Spacing of text will be 1.15.

Glossary of Terms:

Model 3:
Tesla: The company that is behind the Tesla Model 3 as well as various other Tesla’s cars variants.
Autopilot program: Software that enable the Tesla Model 3 to drive without manual control. The software is still in Beta phase.
Autonomous: This refers to driving without manual control. Sensors and cameras will handle objects detection/avoidance.
Sensors: There are many sensors in the Model 3. A sensor is a device used to monitor an action or a condition and respond or provide information. The Model 3 has visual, audio, physical, and chemical sensors throughout different functions of the car.
Tesla Supercharger Station: These refer to another variant of Tesla’s current charging stations. This charging station is different since it support Tesla’s new feature of supercharged car. See “Supercharging” for more details.
Elon Musk: CEO and product architect of Tesla Inc., who is also a part of many other cutting-edge technology companies.
Accessibility: The quality of being easily reached and understood.
Electric Mobility: The accessibility of charging ports in relation to common destinations as well as long distance trips.
Tax Incentives: An electric vehicle incentive in the form of a $7,500 federal income tax credit which is available to US customers who purchase a new Tesla vehicle.
Electric Charge: Range of the car.
Self-Driving: A car that is able to drive itself without constant human input.
Energy Efficient: Technology that is optimized to use the least amount of energy resources.
GPS (Global Positioning System): It is a global navigation satellite system that provides geolocation and time information to a GPS receiver anywhere on Earth.
Acceleration: The rate at which the vehicle can change velocity.
4-Wheel Drive: All four wheels are provided with power dependant on the Tesla’s computer analysis of best power distribution for optimal speed and grip. Only the front two wheels turn.
Carbon Footprint: The average amount of yearly CO2 release from typical Model 3 usage.
Supercharging: This refers to Tesla’s feature introduced alongside with supercharger stations. This feature enable Tesla Model 3 to accelerate itself from 0 to 60 mph under 3 seconds when the car itself is supercharged from these stations.

Colors:

Red, Grey, Black were chosen as our team’s color schemes as they are accessible colors that happen to also be the color of our product’s company.
