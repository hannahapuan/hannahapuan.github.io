Day 0: How to make your first day great!

7:50 AM October 24th, 2017

As a recent grad, I just started my first job as a software engineer at a tech company. Even after a few internships, the step to fulltime is still a new milestone.

Are you ready to start drinking from the firehouse?

<[firehose drinking gif here]>

Day 0:

On my first day, I feel the most important part is to have a structured approach to everything: technical and social challenges.

Learn the Development Environment: Firstly, I tried to learn more about the new software development cycle environment. For me, this was AGILE. There exist great resources on learning the OODA Loop philosophy such as link here.

Use the Development Environment Tools: Secondly, after applying what I know about the software development cycle, I utilize the development tools like Jira. In Jira, I can look at what epics the team is working on currently and get a better idea of the systems the team owns by their referenced links to github repos and associated documentation.

Figure out your role: After getting a general idea of the team's systems through documentation. I reach out to my team. Within the first week or two of starting, I like to schedule some time with each of my coworkers to ask them:

  • what they do
  • what systems they own
  • a brief architectural overview
  • how I can help


    • Permissions!!! An underrated topic is permissions: make sure you have all the permissions you need to the systems you want. This may have been overlooked by whomever sets these settings.
